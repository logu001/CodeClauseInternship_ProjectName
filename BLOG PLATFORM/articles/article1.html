In the 1980s and 1990s, the postmodern order included challenging customs and traditions. Some social scientists questioned whether human development and functioning are characterized by universal laws, and whether development is value- or context-free. The contextualization of human behavior and development within specific action situations, historical periods, and cultural contexts has gained growing recognition. Developmentalists were seriously adopting the idea that culture plays a crucial role in shaping human behavior and development within the framework of these tendencies. This was supported during this period also by the expanding availability of Vygotsky's theories regarding culture and development as a result of fresh translations of his writings and interactions between Russian and Western academics.
